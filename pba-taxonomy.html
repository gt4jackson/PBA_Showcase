Primary categories

The 7 Task Categories

Organized by the dominant modality of evidence production. Tasks may span multiple categories — primary + secondary.

1
CR — Constructed Response
Constructed Response Tasks
Tasks requiring examinees to generate original written or verbal responses, scored against rubrics for content, reasoning, and communication.
Subtypes
  • Extended Written Response (essays, reports, research papers)
  • Short Constructed Response (open-ended explanations)
  • Oral / Verbal Response (presentations, speaking proficiency)
Examples
NAEP Writing AP Free Response GRE Analytical Writing Duolingo Speaking IELTS Speaking Bar Exam Essays
AI Implications

AI-powered AES achieves QWK ~0.68 agreement with human raters [14]; a 2025 synthesis of 65 studies places LLM–human agreement at 0.30–0.80 [15]. LLMs apply rubrics via few-shot prompting; explainability remains a key challenge.

2
PA — Portfolio / Artifact
Portfolio / Artifact-Based Tasks
Tasks where competence evidence is a tangible deliverable created over time. Evaluation focuses on quality, creativity, and technical execution.
Subtypes
  • Portfolio Assessment (curated work collections over time)
  • Project-Based Assessment (multi-phase deliverables)
  • Design Artifact Assessment (prototypes, models, creative works)
Examples
NBPTS Teaching Portfolio ePortfolios in Nursing NGSS Science Projects Capstone Engineering UX/UI Design Portfolios Sustainable City Models
AI Implications

AI supports formative feedback during creation. Emerging challenge: distinguishing human-generated from AI-assisted artifacts. Provenance and process evidence become critical validity considerations.

3
PD — Process / Demonstration
Process / Demonstration Tasks
Tasks where the method of execution is primary — evaluators observe steps, techniques, decision-making, and procedural accuracy.
Subtypes
  • Hands-On Technical Demonstration (lab, technical ops)
  • Clinical / Procedural Performance (medical procedures)
  • Technical Coding / Problem-Solving (live coding, debugging)
Examples
Science Lab Practicals CompTIA Hands-On Labs OSCE Clinical Stations USMLE Step 2 CS HackerRank Projects Google Tech Interviews
AI Implications

AI automates scoring of procedural steps (code execution, surgical metrics). VR-based process assessment enables standardized observation at scale.

4
SB — Simulation-Based
Simulation-Based Tasks
Tasks immersing examinees in realistic scenarios requiring decisions and actions within controlled environments approximating real-world conditions.
Subtypes
  • Standardized Patient / Role-Play Simulations
  • Computer-Based Interactive Simulations (branching scenarios)
  • Conversation-Based Assessment (adaptive dialogue with virtual agents) [4]
  • Virtual / Augmented Reality Simulations
Examples
USMLE Standardized Patients Assessment Center Role-Plays NAEP TEL Tasks ETS CBA Science Inquiry CPA Task-Based Simulations VR Surgical Training
AI Implications

AI-driven scenario generation creates adaptive simulations. Conversation-based assessment uses NLP to adapt dialogue paths in real time [4]. VTREM provides cross-domain VR credibility assessment [13].

5
OB — Observation-Based
Observation-Based Tasks
Tasks assessed through structured expert observation of performance in authentic or semi-authentic settings using standardized protocols.
Subtypes
  • Workplace / Clinical Observation (authentic practice)
  • Structured Behavioral Assessment (assessment centers)
  • Peer / Self-Assessment Protocols (structured peer review)
Examples
ETS PPAT Teaching Obs. Medical Residency Evals Management Assess. Centers Military Leadership Eval. 360-Degree Evaluations Peer Code Reviews
AI Implications

AI video analysis and NLP augment human observers — flagging behaviors, tracking time-on-task, providing structured data from subjective observations.

6
EX — Exhibition / Presentation
Exhibition / Presentation Tasks
Tasks requiring public presentation, performance, or defense of work before an audience or panel. Assesses both work quality and real-time communication.
Subtypes
  • Thesis / Capstone Defense (formal expert panel)
  • Performance Exhibition (artistic, musical, dramatic)
  • Professional Pitch / Presentation (business cases, demos)
Examples
Doctoral Dissertation Defense Music Recitals & Juries Art Exhibitions Business Plan Pitches Science Fair Presentations Hackathon Demos
AI Implications

AI coaching tools analyze speaking pace, filler words, and visual design. AI-powered audience simulation could enable practice at scale before high-stakes panels.

7
AI — AI-Augmented
AI-Augmented Performance Tasks
Cross-cutting category where AI plays a structural role in task design, administration, scoring, or feedback — fundamentally altering the assessment.
Subtypes
  • Adaptive Performance Tasks (AI adjusts difficulty)
  • AI-Scored Performance Tasks (NLP, vision & multimodal scoring)
  • Human-AI Collaborative / Agentic Tasks (examinee directs & supervises AI agents)
  • AI-Generated Feedback Loops (continuous formative cycles)
Examples
Duolingo English Test AI-Adaptive Coding Challenges Automated Essay Scoring HackerRank AI IDE Multi-Agent Writing Feedback AI-Assisted Diagnostic Tasks
Key Considerations

Design for an AI-present world — prioritize process and reasoning over easily generated products [17]. Balance efficiency with explainability; preserve human oversight for high stakes [18]. Address equity; evolve validity frameworks.

Theoretical grounding

Foundation

The classification system is grounded in classical validity theory and extended for the AI era.

Messick (1994) [1]

Distinguished performances vs. products, structured vs. unstructured problems, and contextualized vs. decontextualized skill — foundational categories that map directly to the primary taxonomy.

Linn, Baker & Dunbar (1991) [2]

Established validation criteria: consequences, fairness, transfer, generalizability, cognitive complexity, content quality — the secondary dimensions operationalize several of these.

Authentic Assessment [3]

Tasks should mirror real-world challenges. Higher authenticity increases ecological validity. The Authenticity Level dimension directly encodes Baker, O'Neil & Linn's framework.

Conversation-Based Assessment [4]

Adaptive dialogue with virtual agents elicits evidence about what students know and how they justify understanding — the theoretical basis for Cat 4 Simulation subtypes.

AI-Era Extension

AI reshapes task design, scoring, and feedback. Emerging frameworks fuse classical validity with responsible-AI principles [16] and assume AI is present, favoring process and reasoning evidence [17]. Cat 7 (AI-Augmented) represents this structural shift — not merely a scoring aid but a participant in the assessment itself.
